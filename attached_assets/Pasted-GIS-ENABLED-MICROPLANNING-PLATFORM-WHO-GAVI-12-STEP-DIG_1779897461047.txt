GIS-ENABLED MICROPLANNING PLATFORM
WHO & GAVI 12-STEP DIGITAL MICROPLANNING SYSTEM

Version: 1.0 Prepared For: GIS-Enabled Immunization & Public Health Microplanning Target Users: Healthcare Workers, EPI Teams, District Health Offices, Provincial Teams, National EPI Managers, UNICEF, WHO, Gavi, Implementing Partners Platform: Web + Mobile GIS Application Deployment: Cloud + Offline-Capable Development Platform: Replit / Antigravity

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Traditional immunization and outreach microplanning processes rely heavily on:

paper maps,
static spreadsheets,
incomplete settlement lists,
manual estimations,
fragmented reporting systems.

These approaches often result in:

missed settlements,
inaccurate target populations,
poorly optimized outreach sessions,
inequitable service delivery,
zero-dose children,
inefficient resource allocation.

The GIS-Enabled Microplanning Platform is a next-generation digital microplanning system designed to modernize WHO/Gavi microplanning standards through:

GIS intelligence,
settlement detection,
interactive maps,
spatial population analysis,
automated outreach planning,
intuitive workflows,
offline mobile support,
user-friendly healthcare-worker-centered interfaces.

The platform must transform complex GIS operations into simple, guided workflows usable by healthcare workers with minimal technical skills.

The system should feel:

simple,
visual,
interactive,
mobile-friendly,
engaging,
intelligent,
intuitive,
non-technical.

The application should function as:

“A Digital GIS Assistant for Healthcare Workers.”
