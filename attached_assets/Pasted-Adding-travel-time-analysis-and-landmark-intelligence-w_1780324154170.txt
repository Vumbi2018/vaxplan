Adding travel time analysis and landmark intelligence would elevate VaxPlan from a microplanning tool into a true geospatial decision-support platform.

1. Travel Time is Better Than Distance

Many immunization programmes still use straight-line distance ("as the crow flies"), but that is often misleading.

For example:

Settlement	Distance	Actual Travel Time
A	5 km	15 min (good road)
B	5 km	90 min (river crossing)
C	5 km	2 hours (footpath only)

For planning outreach, travel time is far more useful than distance.

2. How to Calculate Travel Time
Option A: OpenRouteService (Recommended)

Free and open-source.

Website:

OpenRouteService

You send:

{
  "coordinates": [
    [28.308163, -15.430438],
    [28.315000, -15.420000]
  ]
}

Returns:

{
  "distance": 5600,
  "duration": 870
}

Meaning:

Distance = 5.6 km
Travel Time = 14.5 minutes
Option B: OSRM

Very fast and completely free.

OSRM Project

Can be self-hosted.

Ideal if VaxPlan grows nationally.

Option C: Google Maps API

Most accurate in urban areas.

However:

Paid
Costs increase quickly

Better for premium deployments.

8. What WHO and Gavi Would Love

For VaxPlan, the most valuable workflow would be:

Population Raster Detected
        ↓
Estimate Population
        ↓
Find Nearby Landmarks
        ↓
Calculate Travel Time
        ↓
Determine Accessibility
        ↓
Identify Suitable Outreach Location
        ↓
Assign Health Facility
        ↓
Generate Session Plan
        ↓
Forecast Vaccines

This transforms VaxPlan from a GIS mapping tool into a Zero-Dose Identification and Outreach Optimization Platform, directly supporting the goals of reaching missed communities and improving equitable immunization coverage.

A particularly powerful feature would be a map layer called "Unserved Population Clusters", where every raster-derived settlement is automatically ranked by:

Population size.
Travel time to the nearest health facility.
Distance from existing outreach sites.
Presence of community landmarks.
Estimated number of zero-dose children.

That single layer could become the primary planning screen for district and provincial EPI managers.
