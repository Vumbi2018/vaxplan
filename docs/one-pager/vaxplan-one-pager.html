Reach every child. Plan every session. VaxPlan is a mobile-first, offline-capable platform that takes an immunization program from a map of its facilities all the way to a vaccinator's daily itinerary — and back up to a live national coverage dashboard. Built on the WHO RED and Gavi RED-Q strategies.

The problem
Every year millions of children miss life-saving vaccines — not because vaccines don't exist, but because planning breaks down at the last mile. Zero-dose children live in communities that are never reliably mapped; microplans sit in disconnected spreadsheets; denominators are guesswork; and field teams work offline with tools that assume constant internet.
What VaxPlan does
Maps & GISEvery facility, community & catchment, with automatic overlap detection.
Equity targetingFind zero-dose & hard-to-reach children and missed settlements.
Real denominatorsCensus, HMIS, WorldPop & community data with target cohorts.
Needs & budgetsDoses, vials, syringes, safety boxes & wastage, calculated automatically.
MicroplanningGuided RED / RED-Q wizard for routine programs and campaigns.
Field executionDaily itineraries, session capture, defaulters & client logbook.
Live analyticsCoverage, dropout, zero-dose & supervision in real time.
Offline-firstFull functionality with no internet; auto-sync when back online.

Multi-country

One secure platform multiple Ministries use independently, with strict per-country data isolation.

Interoperable

DHIS2 & FHIR R4 adapters connect to existing national health information systems.

Trusted

Role-based access, geographic scoping & full audit logging — auditable end to end.

Built on open foundations & global standards: OpenStreetMap, geoBoundaries, WorldPop, GRID3, UN OCHA/HDX · WHO, UNICEF, Gavi, PAHO & CDC immunization guidance · DHIS2 & FHIR R4.
