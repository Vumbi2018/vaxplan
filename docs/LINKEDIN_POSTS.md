## Long version (story-led — best for credibility)

**Reaching every child shouldn't fail at the last mile. So I built a tool to fix it.**

Every year, millions of children miss routine vaccines. Often it's not a
vaccine-supply problem — it's a *planning* problem:

• Zero-dose children live in communities that are never reliably mapped.
• Microplans sit in spreadsheets, disconnected from maps, populations and stock.
• Denominators are guesswork, so coverage numbers can't be trusted.
• Field teams work offline, but their tools assume constant internet.

Over the past months I've built **VaxPlan** — a mobile-first, offline-capable
GIS microplanning platform for immunization programs. In one system it brings
together:

🗺️ **Maps & GIS** — every facility, community and catchment, with automatic
overlap detection and zero-dose / hard-to-reach targeting.

👥 **Real denominators** — census, HMIS, WorldPop gridded estimates and
community surveys, with automatic target cohorts.

💉 **Vaccine needs & budgets** — doses, vials, syringes, safety boxes and
wastage calculated automatically from tenant-specific schedules.

📋 **Microplanning** — a guided wizard built on the WHO "Reaching Every
District" (RED) and Gavi RED-Q strategies, for both routine and campaigns.

🚶 **Field execution** — daily itineraries (foot, motorbike, boat, air),
session capture, defaulter tracking and a client logbook — all offline-first.

📊 **Live analytics** — coverage, dropout, zero-dose and supervision, in real
time, with hierarchical approvals and full audit logging.

🌍 **Multi-country by design** — one secure, isolated platform multiple
Ministries of Health can use independently, with DHIS2 / FHIR interoperability.

This is my own independent initiative. I built it because I believe the last
mile deserves better tools — and I want it to actually help programs reach
more children.
